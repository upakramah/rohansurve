I recently read an advance reader copy of "How to Build a Billion Dollar Company" by Guillaume Moubeche. The book explores into what sets billion-dollar companies apart, filled with real-world anecdotes. Here are my key takeaways:

**Embracing Paradoxes**
<br/>It spans a wide spectrum of themes and embraces paradoxes that prophesy an all-inclusive environment. For instance, it showcases anecdotes about 'trusting everyone' but later warns about 'trusting no one'. Likewise, it shares exemplars for both 'founder as the face' type as well as the 'founder stays invisible' type.

**Reflective Approach**
<br/>The book reminds me of a similar expedition by Dale Carnegie's with 'How to Win Friends and Influence People'. It is similar not in style, but in its intent to distill patterns from lived experience. Instead of offering rigid formulas, it encourages readers to reflect and draw their own conclusions.

**Standout Quotes**

>The most powerful moves are often the ones no one can defend... until they work.

>Hiring slow doesn't mean fearing growth. It means respecting what you've already built.

>Delegate not just the labor, but the decision-making. It will feel like chaos at first.

>You don't scale by adding people. You scale by adding the right people and removing the wrong ones quickly.

>Coinbase scaled into a $100B company because it was built by people who didn't need to be taught why, only how.
